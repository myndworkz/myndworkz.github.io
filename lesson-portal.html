Lesson Portal: Present Simple vs. Present Continuous

Audio Lesson

Script

This lesson focuses on understanding and using Present Simple and Present Continuous tenses through the context of daily routines and actions happening right now.

  1. Lead-in: Identifying actions in pictures and distinguishing between current actions and routines.
  2. Text Task: Scanning Catherine’s Busy Day to identify verbs and classify them by tense.
  3. Grammar Clarification: Exploring the differences between Present Simple and Present Continuous using examples, timelines, and CCQs.
  4. Controlled Practice: Completing exercises to practice switching between the two tenses.
  5. Free Practice: Discussing daily routines and narrating them using the third person.
  6. Feedback and Wrap-Up: Reviewing key grammar points, correcting errors, and reinforcing pronunciation.

Questions About the Audio

  1. What is Catherine doing at the start of the audio?
  2. What does Catherine do every morning?
  3. How does Catherine travel to work?
  4. What does Catherine usually do in the evenings?
  5. How does the audio describe Catherine’s yoga routine?
  6. What actions are described in Present Continuous tense?
  7. What actions are described in Present Simple tense?

Phrases to Use in Conversation

  • "She wakes up early every morning."
  • "Right now, she is stretching in bed."
  • "She waters her plants every day."
  • "She is riding her bike to work."
  • "In the evening, she reads a book."

Pronunciation and Vocabulary Guidance

Key Pronunciation Tips:

  • Focus on the "-ing" endings in Present Continuous verbs (e.g., "stretching" /ˈstrɛtʃɪŋ/, "watering" /ˈwɔːtərɪŋ/).
  • Emphasize the third-person singular "-s" endings in Present Simple verbs (e.g., "waters" /ˈwɔːtərz/, "rides" /raɪdz/).

Vocabulary Focus:

  • Routine: Actions done regularly, such as "Catherine waters her plants."
  • Temporary: Actions happening now, such as "She is riding her bike."
  • Relax: Unwinding activities like "She reads a book."
